## 1. Implementation Models

### 1.1 Compressed Workweek (4x10)

Employees work the same 40 hours distributed across four days (10 hours per day) instead of five. Pay remains unchanged.

- **Best for:** Manufacturing, shift-based work, operations requiring full-week coverage with rotating teams.
- **Pros:** No reduction in total hours; easier sell to skeptical management; maintains pay parity.
- **Cons:** 10-hour days can increase fatigue; may counteract some wellbeing benefits of a shorter week.
- **Example:** Belgium's 2022 labour law gives workers the right to compress their full-time hours (typically 38 hours) into four days instead of five.

> Source: [Euronews, 2022](https://www.euronews.com/next/2022/02/15/belgium-approves-four-day-week-and-gives-employees-the-right-to-ignore-their-bosses); [4dayweek.io](https://4dayweek.io/country/belgium)

### 1.2 Reduced Hours / 100-80-100 Model (4x8)

The "true" 4-day workweek: employees receive **100% of their pay** for working **80% of the traditional hours** (32 hours instead of 40), while delivering **100% of their previous productivity**.

- **Best for:** Knowledge work, creative industries, professional services.
- **Pros:** Maximum wellbeing gains; forces workflow optimization; strongest recruitment advantage.
- **Cons:** Requires genuine productivity improvements; harder to implement in coverage-dependent roles.
- **This is the model used in most major global trials**, including the UK, US, Ireland, and Australia pilots coordinated by 4 Day Week Global.

> Source: [4 Day Week Global](https://www.4dayweek.com); [MIT Sloan Management Review](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/how-to-implement-a-4-day-workweek-whole-organization)

### 1.3 Flexible / Hybrid Approaches

Rather than mandating a specific day off, companies let teams or individuals choose which day to take off based on workload and coverage needs.

- **Variations include:**
  - **Team choice:** Each team agrees on their day off collectively.
  - **Individual choice:** Employees pick their own day off (with manager approval).
  - **Seasonal or rotating:** The day off rotates weekly or changes by season.
  - **Every-other-week:** Alternating between 4-day and 5-day weeks (a 9/80 schedule variant).

- **Best for:** Client-facing businesses, agencies, companies needing 5-day customer coverage.

> Source: [Deel, 2024](https://www.deel.com/blog/how-to-implement-4-day-workweek/); [4dayweek.io](https://4dayweek.io/advice/how-to-implement-a-4-day-week)

### 1.4 Staggered Schedules

Two or more teams work overlapping four-day schedules to ensure the business operates five (or more) days per week.

- **Example structure:** Team A works Monday-Thursday; Team B works Tuesday-Friday. Wednesday and Thursday are overlap days for collaboration.
- **Results:** A mid-sized retail chain using staggered schedules reported **22% reduced turnover** and **18% higher employee satisfaction** while maintaining consistent store coverage.
- **A production facility** transitioning to four 10-hour shifts with overlapping days reported a **15% productivity increase** due to reduced daily startup times and fewer shift handovers.

> Source: [Myshyft, 2024](https://www.myshyft.com/blog/four-day-workweek-shift-implementation/); [Bonfire Leadership Solutions](https://bonfireleadershipsolutions.com/blog/4-day-workweek-model-strategy/)

## 2. Step-by-Step Implementation

### Phase 1: Assess Organizational Readiness

1. **Evaluate company culture:** Is the organization open to flexibility and change? Cultures that already value work-life balance transition more smoothly.
2. **Audit current workflows:** Identify inefficiencies, excessive meetings, redundant processes, and "time drains" that can be eliminated.
3. **Survey employees:** Conduct surveys and focus groups to gauge interest, concerns, and willingness to change.
4. **Assess operational impact:** Map out how a shorter week affects client services, product delivery, and coverage needs.

> Source: [Deel, 2024](https://www.deel.com/blog/how-to-implement-4-day-workweek/); [4dayweek.io](https://4dayweek.io/advice/how-to-implement-a-4-day-week)

### Phase 2: Design the Model

1. **Choose the right model** (compressed, reduced hours, staggered, flexible) based on your industry and workforce.
2. **Define the work structure:** Is every Friday off? Do teams rotate? Is there a fixed or flexible day off?
3. **Set clear expectations:** Define what "100% productivity" means using outcome-based metrics, not hours.
4. **Create team agreements:** Teams collaboratively define their own rules for communication, meeting schedules, availability windows, and coverage.

> Source: [MIT Sloan Management Review](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/how-to-implement-a-4-day-workweek-whole-organization)

### Phase 3: Run a Pilot

1. **Frame it as an experiment, not a permanent change.** As Andrew Barnes advises: *"Empower your people to come up with the best ideas, because in essence, you're asking them to identify all of those little things that prevent them being as productive as they might be."*
2. **Start small:** Begin with a single department or office for 90 days to 6 months.
3. **Communicate transparently:** Explain the benefits, how feedback will be gathered, what results you're looking for, and when a decision will be made.
4. **Be agile:** The first few weeks are often the most difficult. Adapt policies and procedures based on early findings.

> Source: [MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/how-to-implement-a-4-day-workweek-whole-organization); [Buildremote](https://buildremote.co/four-day-week/4-day-work-week/)

### Phase 4: Measure and Evaluate

Track these key metrics before, during, and after the pilot:

| Metric | How to Measure |
|--------|---------------|
| **Productivity** | Task completion rates, output quality, revenue per employee |
| **Employee wellbeing** | Burnout surveys, stress levels, satisfaction scores |
| **Customer satisfaction** | NPS scores, support response times, client feedback |
| **Absenteeism** | Sick days, unplanned absences |
| **Retention** | Turnover rate, voluntary departures |
| **Financial performance** | Revenue trends, operational costs |

**Pilot results from 91 organizations (3,500 employees) across the UK showed:** 35% revenue increase, higher hiring rates, decreased absenteeism, and 71% of employees reported reduced burnout.

> Source: [MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/how-to-implement-a-4-day-workweek-whole-organization); [Wonderlic](https://wonderlic.com/blog/human-resources/the-essential-guide-to-a-4-day-workweek-insights-results-and-considerations-from-wonderlics-experience/)

### Phase 5: Implement Permanently (or Iterate)

1. **Document and share results** with leadership, boards, and stakeholders using objective data.
2. **Foster cross-department collaboration:** Establish sessions where teams share successful strategies.
3. **Evolve the model:** Germany's 2024 pilot follow-up found that 22% of companies further adapted their model for greater flexibility. Long-term success depends on an organization's ability to redesign work in response to real-world conditions.

> Source: [4 Day Week Global - Germany Results](https://www.4dayweek.com/germany-2024-pilot-results)

## 3. Common Challenges and Solutions

### 3.1 Customer and Client Coverage

**Challenge:** Clients and customers expect five-day (or more) availability.

**Solutions:**
- **Staggered schedules:** Team A (Mon-Thu) + Team B (Tue-Fri) provides five-day coverage with individual four-day weeks.
- **Core overlap days:** Ensure all teams are present on critical collaboration days.
- **AI and automation:** Invest in chatbots and self-service tools for basic inquiries during off-hours.
- **Proactive communication:** Inform clients of the new schedule and set clear response-time expectations.

**Evidence:** Bolt reported that while 84% of employees saw improved work-life balance, nearly 15% in sales encountered "external-facing challenges" with client scheduling -- solvable with staggered coverage.

> Source: [Great Place to Work](https://www.greatplacetowork.com/resources/blog/the-four-day-work-week-debate); [HR Daily Advisor, 2025](https://hrdailyadvisor.hci.org/2025/02/27/which-companies-have-embraced-the-4-day-work-week/)

### 3.2 Management Resistance

**Challenge:** Leaders worry about loss of control, reduced output, and accountability.

**Solutions:**
- **Lead with data:** Present results from the 500+ companies that have trialed the model (92% continued afterward).
- **Shift to outcome-based management:** Evaluate performance on deliverables and impact, not hours present.
- **Start with a pilot:** Frame it as a time-limited experiment with clear success criteria, reducing perceived risk.
- **Build trust:** Andrew Barnes warns against mixed messaging: *"What you don't want them to hear is that pay is going to go down and you're going to want them to do more with less."*

> Source: [MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/how-to-implement-a-4-day-workweek-whole-organization)

### 3.3 Meeting Culture Overload

**Challenge:** Too many meetings fill the available time, making a 4-day week feel impossible.

**Solutions:**
- **Cut meeting durations:** Microsoft Japan capped meetings at 30 minutes (down from 60), and nearly half of all meetings adopted this standard.
- **Reduce meeting frequency:** Audit all standing meetings; eliminate those that don't add clear value.
- **Limit attendees:** Invite only necessary participants; share notes with others.
- **Protect deep work time:** Institute "no-meeting" blocks (2-4 hours) for uninterrupted focus.
- **Default to asynchronous:** Replace status-update meetings with written updates (Slack, Notion, Loom).

**Data:** Employees spend 21.5 hours weekly in meetings on average. 47% consider meetings their biggest time-waster. Workers are interrupted 56 times daily.

> Source: [Britannica ProCon](https://www.britannica.com/procon/four-day-workweek-debate); [NPR, 2019](https://www.npr.org/2019/11/04/776163853/microsoft-japan-says-4-day-workweek-boosted-workers-productivity-by-40)

### 3.4 Workplace Inequity

**Challenge:** Some roles (developers, writers) adapt easily; others (helpdesk, front-line staff) may not get the same flexibility.

**Solutions:**
- **Role-specific models:** Use compressed (4x10) schedules for coverage roles and reduced hours (4x8) for knowledge roles.
- **Staggered coverage teams:** Ensure shift-based workers still get a day off each week through rotation.
- **Equal treatment in spirit:** Even if the model differs by role, the principle of an extra day off should apply to everyone.

> Source: [Great Place to Work](https://www.greatplacetowork.com/resources/blog/the-four-day-work-week-debate)

### 3.5 Work Intensity and Boundary-Setting

**Challenge:** Compressing work into fewer days can increase stress if not managed properly.

**Solutions:**
- **Redesign work, don't compress it:** The goal is eliminating waste, not working faster. Audit and remove low-value activities.
- **Set clear boundaries:** Employees should not feel obligated to respond on their day off.
- **Monitor intensity:** Juliet Schor's research found that employees in successful trials *did not* report an increase in work intensity, suggesting the work reorganization strategy succeeded.

> Source: [Boston College / Juliet Schor](https://www.bc.edu/bc-web/bcnews/nation-world-society/sociology/-study-pilots-four-day-work-week.html)

## 4. Counterarguments and Rebuttals

### "Productivity will drop"

**The data says otherwise:**
- **Microsoft Japan (2019):** 40% productivity increase during their 4-day week trial with 2,300 employees. ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2019/11/04/776163853/microsoft-japan-says-4-day-workweek-boosted-workers-productivity-by-40))
- **UK Pilot (2022):** 91 companies, 3,500 employees -- 35% revenue increase during the trial period. ([4 Day Week Global](https://www.4dayweek.com/uk-pilot-results))
- **Australia (2023):** 70% of employers said productivity was higher; 30% said it was the same. No employer reported a decline. ([4 Day Week Global](https://www.4dayweek.com/research))
- **Henley Business School (UK):** 77% of workers reported increased productivity on a 4-day week. ([Henley Business School](https://www.henley.ac.uk))
- **Parkinson's Law:** Work expands to fill the time available. Fewer hours force prioritization and efficiency.

**Juliet Schor (Boston College):** *"What really surprised me was that people feel so much more productive, and the companies are saying people are more productive, too. The big jump in self-reported productivity is pretty striking."*

### "Not all industries can do it"

**Diverse industries are succeeding:**
- **Finance:** Atom Bank (UK digital bank) implemented a 4-day, 34-hour week in 2021 with no decline in service or efficiency. ([Fortune, 2021](https://fortune.com/2021/11/23/fintech-atom-bank-moves-4-day-work-week-without-cutting-pay/))
- **Tech:** Buffer made a permanent 4-day week after a 2020 pilot with stable productivity and decreased stress. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2021/10/22/these-companies-have-a-4-day-workweek-heres-what-theyve-learned.html))
- **Crowdfunding:** Kickstarter transitioned in 2022 with improved morale and no negative business impact. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/kickstarter-ceo-stands-4-day-160428523.html))
- **E-commerce:** Bolt reported 84% improved work-life balance and 91% of managers favored continuing. ([Great Place to Work](https://www.greatplacetowork.com/resources/blog/the-four-day-work-week-debate))
- **Government:** Iceland's pilot (2015-2019) led to 51% of the workforce transitioning to shorter weeks by 2022. ([Euronews, 2024](https://www.euronews.com/next/2024/02/02/the-four-day-week-which-countries-have-embraced-it-and-how-s-it-going-so-far))
- **Manufacturing:** Factories using 4x10 schedules with rotating teams maintain output while giving workers extended weekends. ([Shyfter](https://shyfter.co/en/blog/the-4-day-workweek-revolution-which-industries-are-making-it-work-globally))
- **Healthcare:** A 35,000-person hospital chain in New Jersey was part of Juliet Schor's research cohort. Administrative departments and clinics have successfully adopted 32-hour weeks. ([BC Magazine, 2025](https://www.bc.edu/bc-web/sites/bc-magazine/summer-2025-issue/linden-lane/a-new-way-to-work--.html))

### "Clients need us five days a week"

**Coverage solutions exist:**
- **Staggered teams** provide five-day (or more) client coverage while individual employees work four days.
- **92% of participating UK companies maintained or improved service levels** during the trial. ([4 Day Week Global](https://www.4dayweek.com/uk-pilot-results))
- Bolt's experience: even in sales, external-facing challenges were manageable and solvable.
- AI-powered chatbots and self-service tools handle basic inquiries on off-days.

### "It's just a trend that will fade"

**The momentum is accelerating:**
- **2018:** Perpetual Guardian (NZ) runs the first major trial.
- **2019:** Microsoft Japan's 40% productivity boost makes global headlines.
- **2022:** UK runs the world's largest pilot (61 companies); 89% kept the policy afterward.
- **2024:** Germany, Portugal, Brazil, South Africa run national-level pilots.
- **2025:** Tokyo introduces 4-day weeks for government employees; Spain proposes 37.5-hour week law.
- **2026:** Over 2.7 million UK workers (11% of the workforce) work a 4-day week. Sweden and Norway launch pilots.
- **92% of companies that try it keep it.** This is not a fad; it's a structural shift.

> Source: [4 Day Week Global](https://www.4dayweek.com/research); [World Economic Forum, 2025](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2025/10/four-day-week-work-jobs-and-skills/)

### "Employees will just slack off"

**The evidence shows the opposite:**
- **Turnover dropped 57%** in UK pilot companies. Employees are *more* engaged, not less. ([Great Place to Work](https://www.greatplacetowork.com/resources/blog/the-four-day-work-week-debate))
- **15% of employees said no amount of money would make them return to a 5-day schedule** at another company. ([Workbuzz](https://workbuzz.com/blog/impact-of-a-four-day-work-week-on-recruitment-and-retention/))
- **65% reduction in sick days** during the UK trial. ([Britannica ProCon](https://www.britannica.com/procon/four-day-workweek-debate))
- The model creates a *social contract*: employees self-organize and hold each other accountable because everyone benefits from the shared reward. As Andrew Barnes notes: *"I need you to succeed in order for me to get the time off that I want."*

### "It will increase burnout, not reduce it"

**Rebuttal -- but this concern has nuance:**
- The concern is valid for **compressed (4x10) models**, where 10-hour days can increase fatigue.
- For the **100-80-100 model** (32 hours, full pay), burnout *decreases significantly*:
  - **62% of UK trial employees** reported reduced burnout.
  - **39% reported reduced stress.**
  - **Wellbeing improvements were sustained a full year after the trial began**, ruling out novelty effects.
- **Critical distinction:** The goal is to *redesign work*, not *compress* it. Eliminating waste (meetings, interruptions, redundant processes) is what makes 32 productive hours possible.

> Source: [Nature Human Behaviour, 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-025-02259-6); [APA Monitor, 2025](https://www.apa.org/monitor/2025/01/rise-of-4-day-workweek.html)

### "Flexibility matters more than a fixed day off"

**Rebuttal -- why not both?**
- A Gallup poll found that "the quality of the work experience has 2.5x to 3x the impact of the number of days or hours worked." This is true but not contradictory.
- The 4-day workweek *is itself a form of flexibility*. Many implementations allow teams to choose their own day off.
- The structured day off provides a *universal floor* of flexibility, unlike ad-hoc remote-work policies that depend on manager goodwill.
- **89% of employees who support a 4-day week would sacrifice something at work for it** -- indicating it addresses a deep preference, not just scheduling convenience.

> Source: [Britannica ProCon](https://www.britannica.com/procon/four-day-workweek-debate); [Drive Research](https://www.driveresearch.com/market-research-company-blog/4-day-workweek-survey/)

## 6. Industries Where It's Harder (and Creative Solutions)

### Healthcare

**Why it's harder:** Patient care demands 24/7 availability. Reducing hours risks compromising care quality.

**Creative solutions found:**
- **Administrative and research departments** have successfully adopted 32-hour weeks.
- **Specialized clinics** (non-emergency) can implement reduced hours.
- **Nursing staff** experiment with longer shifts over fewer days (already common in many hospitals with 3x12 schedules).
- **A 35,000-person hospital chain in New Jersey** participated in Juliet Schor's 4-day week research study, demonstrating that even large healthcare systems can adapt.
- **Key insight:** Not every role in healthcare is patient-facing; significant portions of the healthcare workforce (administration, IT, HR, research, billing) can transition.

> Source: [BC Magazine, 2025](https://www.bc.edu/bc-web/sites/bc-magazine/summer-2025-issue/linden-lane/a-new-way-to-work--); [Shyfter](https://shyfter.co/en/blog/the-4-day-workweek-revolution-which-industries-are-making-it-work-globally)

### Emergency Services

**Why it's harder:** Fire, police, and ambulance services require round-the-clock staffing.

**Creative solutions:**
- Many emergency services already use **alternative schedules** (24-on/48-off, 4-on/4-off rotations) that provide more days off than a standard 5-day week.
- The 4-day week conversation for emergency services is about **extending existing shift flexibility**, not starting from scratch.

### Retail and Hospitality

**Why it's harder:** Stores and restaurants depend on consumer accessibility. Reduced hours could mean reduced revenue.

**Creative solutions:**
- **Overlapping team schedules** and strategic staffing maintain full operating hours while individual employees work 4-day weeks.
- **A mid-sized retail chain** reported 22% reduced turnover and 18% higher satisfaction using staggered schedules.
- **Key insight:** Well-rested staff provide better customer experiences, potentially increasing per-visit sales even if individual hours decrease.
- The point is not to close the store on Fridays -- it is to ensure no *individual employee* works more than four days per week.

> Source: [Myshyft](https://www.myshyft.com/blog/four-day-workweek-shift-implementation/); [Great Place to Work](https://www.greatplacetowork.com/resources/blog/the-four-day-work-week-debate)

### Manufacturing

**Why it's harder:** Production lines, equipment utilization, and supply chain coordination create constraints. Error rates may increase under time pressure.

**Creative solutions:**
- **Four 10-hour days** (compressed model) is widely used in manufacturing, maintaining 40-hour output.
- **Rotating teams** maintain continuous production while individual workers get shorter weeks.
- **Highly automated facilities** with sophisticated quality control adapt more easily.
- **A production facility** reported 15% productivity increase after transitioning to 4x10 with overlapping days, due to fewer shift handovers and startup times.

> Source: [Shyfter](https://shyfter.co/en/blog/the-4-day-workweek-revolution-which-industries-are-making-it-work-globally); [Myshyft](https://www.myshyft.com/blog/four-day-workweek-shift-implementation/)

## 7. Legal and Regulatory Considerations

### Belgium: Right to a 4-Day Week (November 2022)

Belgium became the first country to enshrine a **right to request** a 4-day workweek in law, as part of its broader "Labour Deal."

**Key provisions:**
- Full-time workers can request to work their usual hours (typically 38/week) across 4 days instead of 5 (compressed model, ~9.5 hours/day).
- The scheme is **voluntary for both parties**: employees can request it, and employers can refuse -- but only with written, substantiated reasons.
- Employees who request a 4-day week are **protected against dismissal** based on that request.
- Employees cannot be treated adversely for making the request.
- Applies to all employers with more than 20 staff.
- Also includes a **right to disconnect** outside working hours.

**Important caveat:** Belgium's model is a *compressed* week (same hours, fewer days), not a *reduced hours* model. Pay remains unchanged because total hours remain unchanged.

> Source: [American Bar Association, 2023](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/labor_law/publications/ilelc_newsletters/issue-winter-2023/belgium-approves-four-day-work-week/); [KPMG Law Belgium](https://www.kpmglaw.be/en/news/four-day-alternating-work-week-not-a-mere-discretionary-possibility-of-the-employer/); [Euronews, 2022](https://www.euronews.com/next/2022/02/15/belgium-approves-four-day-week-and-gives-employees-the-right-to-ignore-their-bosses)

### Spain: 37.5-Hour Workweek Bill (2025)

- In May 2025, the Spanish government approved a bill to reduce the standard full-time workweek from 40 to 37.5 hours without cutting pay. It still requires parliamentary approval.
- Expected to impact over **12 million private-sector workers**.
- Spain also invested **50 million euros** into a 3-year 4-day workweek trial, covering 100% of participating companies' costs in year one, 50% in year two, and 33% in year three.
- A separate **9.65 million euro** initiative targets small- and medium-sized industrial companies.

> Source: [Euronews, 2025](https://www.euronews.com/next/2025/05/07/spain-moves-forward-with-a-proposed-law-to-shorten-the-working-work-to-375-hours); [Time](https://time.com/5948677/four-day-work-week-spain/)

### Iceland: National Shift to Shorter Hours (2015-2022)

- Government-run pilot (2015-2019): 2,500 workers tested 35-36 hour weeks at full pay.
- Deemed a success; Icelandic trade unions negotiated broader reductions.
- By 2022, **over 51% of the country's workforce** had transitioned to shorter working weeks.
- Not a single legislative mandate, but rather a union-driven movement supported by pilot evidence.

> Source: [Euronews, 2024](https://www.euronews.com/next/2024/02/02/the-four-day-week-which-countries-have-embraced-it-and-how-s-it-going-so-far)

### Japan: Government Employee 4-Day Week (2025)

- Tokyo introduced 4-day weeks for government employees starting April 2025.
- Aims to address declining fertility rates (1.2 in 2023) and "karoshi" (death by overwork).
- Also targets gender workforce disparities (women's participation: 55% vs. men's: 72%).

> Source: [HR Asia](https://hr.asia/featured-news/10-countries-experimenting-with-a-4-day-work-week/); [CEOWORLD, 2025](https://ceoworld.biz/2025/02/20/countries-leading-the-4-day-work-week-movement/)

### Germany: National Pilot (2023-2024, with 2026 Follow-Up)

- 45 organizations participated, in collaboration with Intraprenor consultancy and University of Munster.
- 40% of participants introduced the pilot within selected teams first.
- **2026 follow-up:** 70% of organizations continue with some form of reduced working time; 22% further adapted their model for flexibility.
- Financial indicators remained stable; improvements in life satisfaction, sleep quality, and physical activity.

> Source: [4 Day Week Global - Germany Results](https://www.4dayweek.com/germany-2024-pilot-results)

### Other Regulatory Developments

- **Portugal (2024):** Government-funded 4-day week trial.
- **Brazil (2024):** South America's first coordinated 4-day week trial.
- **Sweden and Norway (2026):** Joint pilot program launched.
- **2026 is expected to be a turning point** for the 4-day workweek across Europe, with more governments launching national pilot schemes.

> Source: [Protime](https://www.protime.eu/en/news/4-day-work-week-eu); [4 Day Week Global](https://www.4dayweek.com/research)

## 8. Expert Opinions and Advocates

### Andrew Barnes -- Founder, 4 Day Week Global

**Background:** New Zealand entrepreneur; founder of Perpetual Guardian, the country's largest corporate trustee company. Initiated the modern 4-day workweek movement with a landmark trial in 2018.

**The Perpetual Guardian Trial (2018):**
- 240 employees given an extra day off weekly at full pay for 8 weeks.
- **Results:** Productivity up 20%, stress down, customer engagement up 30%, work-life balance satisfaction jumped from 54% to 78%.
- Made permanent; inspired the founding of 4 Day Week Global.

**Key quote on implementation:** *"Empower your people to come up with the best ideas, because in essence, you're asking them to identify all of those little things that prevent them being as productive as they might be."*

**Key quote on trust:** *"What you don't want them to hear is that pay is going to go down and you're going to want them to do more with less."*

**Key quote on accountability:** *"I need you to succeed in order for me to get the time off that I want."* (on the self-reinforcing social contract the model creates)

> Source: [4 Day Week Global](https://www.4dayweek.com/andrew-barnes); [MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/how-to-implement-a-4-day-workweek-whole-organization); [World Economic Forum, 2018](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2018/07/four-day-work-week-productivity-new-zealand-trial/)

### Juliet Schor -- Professor of Sociology, Boston College

**Background:** Economist and sociologist; lead researcher for the largest global 4-day workweek study. Author of *"Four Days a Week"* and previous bestseller *"The Overworked American."*

**Research scope:** Studied 245 organizations and 8,700 employees across seven countries (Ireland, UK, US, Canada, South Africa, New Zealand, Australia) beginning in 2022. Organizations ranged from a 35,000-person hospital chain to a small craft brewery.

**Key findings:**
- Massive wellbeing improvements: reduced burnout, more positive emotions, better mental and physical health.
- Employees did *not* report increased work intensity, suggesting work reorganization succeeded.
- After one year, over 90% of companies were still on a 4-day week.
- Women did not simply do more housework on their day off. Men also did more housework and especially more childcare, and the division of unpaid labor improved.

**Key quote:** *"What really surprised me was that people feel so much more productive, and the companies are saying people are more productive, too. The big jump in self-reported productivity is pretty striking. Beyond maintaining productivity, people just feel so much better."*

**Senate testimony:** Schor testified before the U.S. Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions in March 2024, presenting evidence from global trials.

> Source: [Boston College](https://www.bc.edu/bc-web/bcnews/nation-world-society/sociology/-study-pilots-four-day-work-week.html); [BC Magazine, 2025](https://www.bc.edu/bc-web/sites/bc-magazine/summer-2025-issue/linden-lane/a-new-way-to-work--); [U.S. Senate HELP Committee, 2024](https://www.help.senate.gov/download/dr-schor-help-testimony-3-14-24pdf)

### Alex Soojung-Kim Pang -- Author, Global Programs Director at 4 Day Week Global

**Background:** Silicon Valley-based futurist and consultant with two decades studying people, technology, and work. Has held academic positions at Stanford, UC Berkeley, and Oxford. Currently Global Programs Director at 4 Day Week Global.

**Books:**
- *"Shorter: Work Better, Smarter, and Less -- Here's How"* (Public Affairs, 2020) -- Documents how companies move to 4-day weeks without cutting salaries or profits.

**Core thesis:** By reducing distractions, eliminating inefficiencies, and creating time for high-quality focus and collaboration, 4-day workweeks boost recruitment and retention, make leaders more thoughtful and companies more sustainable, and improve work-life balance.

## 9. Environmental Benefits

The 4-day workweek offers significant environmental co-benefits, often overlooked in the productivity debate.

### Commuting Reduction

- Commuting time per week dropped from 3.5 hours to 2.6 hours (**27% reduction**) in trials.
- Car commuting fell from 56.5% to 52.5% of employees.
- **Greenpeace estimated** the 4-day week could cut commuting miles by **558 million per week**.

### Carbon Emissions

- **Autonomy (UK think tank):** A shorter workweek could reduce the UK's carbon footprint by up to **127 million tonnes**, equivalent to removing 27 million cars from the road.
- **2012 study:** A 10% reduction in work hours leads to declines of **12.1% in ecological footprint**, **14.6% in carbon footprint**, and **4.2% in CO2 emissions**.
- **Stop The Clock (2021):** Adopting a 4-day workweek could reduce the UK's carbon footprint by up to **21.3%**.

### Real-World Results

- **Microsoft Japan (2019):** 23% decrease in electricity costs.
- **Valencia, Spain (2024):** 58% reduction in nitrogen dioxide (NO2) emissions during a trial involving 360,000 workers.
- **Boston College / 4 Day Week Global:** Shorter workweeks linked to increased cycling and walking.

> Source: [4 Day Week Global](https://www.4dayweek.com/news-posts/environmental-benefits-2hxgl); [Euronews Green, 2025](https://www.euronews.com/green/2025/01/29/work-less-save-co2-how-a-four-day-working-week-can-benefit-the-planet); [Greenpeace Aotearoa](https://www.greenpeace.org/aotearoa/story/could-a-4-day-working-week-help-reduce-emissions-and-our-carbon-footprint/)
