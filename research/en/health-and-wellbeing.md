## 1. Mental Health Improvements

### Stress Reduction

- **39% of employees reported reduced stress** during the UK 4-day workweek pilot involving 2,900 employees across 61 companies (Autonomy Institute / University of Cambridge, 2023).
- **78% of employees feel happier and less stressed** after switching to a 4-day workweek (Henley Business School study).
- **33% decrease in work-related stress** reported by pilot programme participants across multiple trials (4 Day Week Global).
- **35% reduction in stress levels** reported during the Valencia, Spain trial of ~360,000 workers (Valencia City Council, 2023).
- **Mental health score increased from 2.95 to 3.32** (on a standardised scale) during the UK pilot (Autonomy Institute, 2023).
- **Anxiety scores decreased from 2.26 to 1.96** during the UK pilot (Autonomy Institute, 2023).
- **54% of employees reported a reduction in negative emotions** across global trials (4 Day Week Global).

### Long-Term Sustainability of Mental Health Gains

- One year after the UK 2022 trial, **improvements in mental health, physical health, and burnout had been maintained**, and 89% of employers remained on reduced hours (NPR / 4 Day Week Global, 2024).
- Benefits from the 6-country Nature study **held fairly stable in a 12-month follow-up** (Nature Human Behaviour, 2025).

### Anxiety and Depression

- Levels of **anxiety, fatigue, and sleep issues all decreased** across the UK pilot, while mental and physical health improved (ScienceDaily / University of Cambridge, 2023).
- Burnout, stress, and anxiety were **reduced more significantly for women than men** during the 4 Day Week Global trial across 6 countries and 33 companies (Women's Budget Group / 4 Day Week Global).

## 2. Physical Health Benefits

### Exercise and Physical Activity

- Employees increased exercise by an average of **23.7 additional minutes per week**, with individual sessions increasing by approximately 6 minutes on average (4 Day Week Global Foundation).
- **37% of participants noted a rise in physical activity** during the Valencia, Spain trial (Valencia City Council, 2023).
- Non-profit and professional service employees showed the **largest increase in time spent exercising** among sectors in the UK pilot (Autonomy Institute, 2023).

### Sleep Quality Improvements

- **Sleep problems decreased by 8%** across trial participants (4 Day Week Global).
- **64% of participants increased the amount of sleep** they got during the Valencia trial (Valencia City Council, 2023).
- **Nearly 40% said it was easier to get to sleep** after switching to a 4-day week, and nearly 50% said they were less tired than before (4 Day Week Global).
- Construction and manufacturing workers showed the **biggest reduction in sleep problems** in the UK pilot (Autonomy Institute, 2023).
- Three key mechanisms explained improvements: **better sleep quality, reduced fatigue, and increased perceived work ability** (Nature Human Behaviour, 2025).

### Overall Health Outcomes

- Analysis of **2,896 employees across 141 organisations** in six countries found statistically significant improvements in **physical health** compared to 12 control companies that saw no change (Nature Human Behaviour, Prunster et al., 2025).
- Physical health improvements, while smaller in magnitude than mental health gains, were nonetheless statistically significant and sustained at 12-month follow-up (Nature Human Behaviour, 2025).
- **65% reduction in sick days** taken during the UK pilot compared to the same period the prior year (Autonomy Institute / University of Cambridge, 2023).
- **62% reduction in staff sick leave** for companies transitioning to a 4-day model (4dayweek.io compilation).

## 3. Work-Life Balance

### Family Time

- **Time spent by male workers looking after their children increased by 27%** during the UK 4-day workweek trial (Autonomy Institute, 2023).
- **60% of employees found an increased ability to combine paid work with care responsibilities** (Autonomy Institute / UK pilot, 2023).
- **54% found it easier to balance work with household jobs** (Autonomy Institute / UK pilot, 2023).
- **62% reported it easier to combine work with social life** (Autonomy Institute / UK pilot, 2023).

### Personal Development and Hobbies

- **72% of participants in the Valencia trial devoted more time to friends** (Valencia City Council, 2023).
- Many workers reported feeling **more energised and having more energy for activities such as exercise, friends, and hobbies** (Iceland trial, Autonomy Institute, 2021).
- Employees redirected the extra day toward **personal organisation and household tasks**, freeing weekends for recreation, family time, and hobbies (UK pilot, 2023).

### Commuting Reduction

- Weekly commuting time dropped from **3.5 hours to 2.6 hours -- a 27% reduction** (UK pilot, 2023).
- Overall reduction in employees commuting by car, from **56.5% to 52.5%** (UK pilot, 2023).

### Caregiving

- **60% of employees** found an increased ability to combine paid work with care responsibilities in the UK pilot (Autonomy Institute, 2023).
- The 4-day week is particularly significant for the **72% of working women who juggle work with primary caregiving responsibilities** (University of Queensland, 2023).
- **90% of caregivers** for older people and those with disabilities are women, making reduced work schedules particularly impactful for this group (University of Queensland, 2023).

### Work-Life Balance Scores

- At Perpetual Guardian (New Zealand), work-life balance satisfaction jumped from **54% to 78%** after the trial (Perpetual Guardian / University of Auckland, 2018).
- **78% of employees** in a broader survey believe shortening the workweek would positively impact work-life balance (4dayweek.io).
- **84% of employees** reported improved work-life balance across multiple trials, and 86% said they were more efficient with their time (4 Day Week Global).

## 4. Gender Equality Impact

### Redistribution of Caregiving and Housework

- Men who worked a 4-day week spent **22% more time on childcare** and **23% more time on housework** during a trial across 6 countries and 33 companies (4 Day Week Global / Women's Budget Group).
- Australian men who trialled the 4-day workweek **spent more time on childcare and housework, while women's share of these responsibilities decreased** (University of Queensland, 2023).
- Time spent by male workers looking after their children **increased by 27%** in the UK pilot (Autonomy Institute, 2023).

### Differential Benefits for Women

- Burnout, stress, and anxiety were **reduced more significantly for women than for men** during global trials (4 Day Week Global / Women's Budget Group).
- **Women are 5% more likely than men** to favour a 4-day workweek, partly due to childcare cost savings (4dayweek.io).
- The 4-day week addresses a key barrier: women currently do **50% more unpaid work than men** on average, spending twice as much time on cooking, childcare, and housework (Women's Budget Group).

### Women's Workforce Participation

- Flexible work arrangements like the 4-day week are considered crucial for **retaining and engaging women in the workforce**; when rather than where flexibility matters most (MSNBC / 4 Day Week Campaign).
- The gender pension gap is stark -- women retire with average savings of **GBP 69,000 vs. GBP 205,000 for men** (a 92% gap) -- making equitable work arrangements that support women's careers essential (Women's Budget Group).
- Nearly **47% of menstruating women experience severe period pain most months**, and over one-third feel unable to be transparent about period-related sick days -- a shorter week provides built-in flexibility (Women's Budget Group).

### Important Caveats

- The 4-day workweek is **not a "silver bullet"** for gender equality; it must be accompanied by robust policies including universal childcare, equal parental leave, and cultural change (University of Queensland / Latinoamerica21).
- If predominantly women adopt shorter hours, the policy risks being **stigmatised** and could work against equality; universal adoption by both genders and at all seniority levels is critical (People Management / The Persistent).

## 5. Employee Satisfaction and Happiness

### Trial Ratings and Preference

- Employees rated their 4-day week experience **9.1 out of 10** on average across global trials (4 Day Week Global).
- Companies rated the trial experience at an average of **9 out of 10** (APA Monitor, 2025).
- **92.1% of Microsoft Japan employees** said they liked the 4-day workweek (Microsoft Japan, 2019).
- **More than 9 out of 10 employees** wanted to continue with the 4-day week after participating in global trials (4 Day Week Global).
- **15% of employees stated no amount of money would convince them** to return to a 5-day week (UK pilot / Autonomy Institute, 2023).
- **58% of employees would choose a 4-day workweek over a salary increase** (4dayweek.io).
- **66% of employees** view a shorter workweek as an appealing benefit when considering jobs (4dayweek.io).

### Company Continuation Rates

- **92% of UK pilot companies** (56 of 61) continued the 4-day week after the trial, with 18 making it permanent (Autonomy Institute, 2023).
- **Over 90% of companies** in the 141-organisation Nature study retained the 4-day arrangement post-trial (Nature Human Behaviour, 2025).
- **73% of German companies** that tried a 4-day workweek chose to keep the schedule (HR Brew / 4 Day Week Global, 2024).
- **70% of German pilot organisations** continued with some form of reduced working time at 2-year follow-up (4 Day Week Global, 2026).

### Employee Retention

- **57% reduction in employee turnover** during the UK pilot (Autonomy Institute, 2023).
- **68% of businesses** experienced greater success attracting and retaining talent (4dayweek.io).

### Happiness and Life Satisfaction

- Workers in the Valencia trial reported feeling **happier and more personally satisfied**, with higher self-perceived health status (Valencia City Council, 2023).
- The German trial found **significant positive changes in life satisfaction**, mainly due to additional free time (University of Munster / 4 Day Week Global, 2024).
- British Telecom research showed workers generated **approximately 13% more sales** during weeks when reporting higher happiness levels (4 Day Week Global / British Telecom).

## 6. Burnout Prevention

### Burnout Reduction Statistics

- **71% of UK pilot employees reported reduced burnout** at the end of the 6-month trial (Autonomy Institute / University of Cambridge, 2023).
- **62% of employees reported experiencing burnout "often" or "extremely often"** under the traditional 5-day model (comparison baseline from 4dayweek.io).
- In a healthcare-specific study of 38 healthcare leaders, burnout **decreased from 61% to just 4%** after implementing a 4-day workweek (PubMed / U.S. hospital study, 2024).

### Healthcare Leader Burnout Study (In Detail)

- **Burnout**: Dropped from 61% to 4%
- **Job satisfaction**: Increased from 71% to 96%
- **Joy in work**: Rose from 34% to 86%
- Improvements were **sustained at 6-month and 12-month follow-up** with no changes in patient safety, satisfaction, or hospital-acquired conditions (PubMed, 2024).

### Burnout Across Global Trials

- The largest international study (2,896 employees, 141 organisations, 6 countries) found **statistically significant reductions in burnout** compared to control companies that showed no change (Nature Human Behaviour, 2025).
- Burnout and job satisfaction showed the **most significant improvements** among all measured outcomes, larger than physical health improvements (Nature Human Behaviour, 2025).
- Improvements in burnout first appeared **within the first three months** and were maintained through month six and beyond (Scientific American, 2023).

### Dose-Response Relationship

- **Greater reductions in hours worked predict larger improvements** in subjective wellbeing; reductions of 8+ hours yielded the most significant benefits, though smaller reductions also showed positive effects (Nature Human Behaviour, 2025).

## 7. Specific Study Results by Trial

### Iceland (2015-2019)

- **Scale**: 2,500 workers (over 1% of Iceland's working population) in government workplaces
- **Model**: Reduced from 40 to 35-36 hours/week, no pay reduction
- **Wellbeing**: Worker wellbeing "dramatically increased" across stress, burnout, health, and work-life balance indicators
- **Stress**: Reduced for workers at trial workplaces; control workplaces saw no change
- **Satisfaction**: 78% of workers were satisfied with working time; 62% of those with reduced hours were more satisfied
- **Productivity**: Maintained or improved across majority of trial workplaces
- **Adoption**: 86% of the country's workforce now work shorter hours or have gained the right to (Autonomy Institute, 2021; updated 2024)
- **Source**: [Autonomy Institute -- Going Public: Iceland's Journey to a Shorter Working Week](https://autonomy.work/portfolio/icelandsww/)

### UK Pilot (June-December 2022)

- **Scale**: ~2,900 employees across 61 companies in diverse sectors
- **Model**: 4-day, 32-hour week with no pay reduction
- **Stress**: 39% reported less stress
- **Burnout**: 71% reported reduced burnout
- **Sick days**: 65% reduction
- **Staff turnover**: 57% reduction
- **Work-life balance**: 54% found household tasks easier; 60% improved care/work balance; 62% improved social/work balance
- **Mental health score**: Increased from 2.95 to 3.32
- **Anxiety score**: Decreased from 2.26 to 1.96
- **Revenue**: Increased 1.4% on average
- **Continuation**: 92% of companies continued; 18 made it permanent
- **1-year follow-up**: Improvements maintained; 89% of employers still on reduced hours
- **Source**: [Autonomy Institute -- The Results Are In](https://autonomy.work/portfolio/uk4dwpilotresults/)

### Nature Human Behaviour International Study (Published 2025)

- **Scale**: 2,896 employees across 141 organisations in 6 countries (US, Canada, UK, Ireland, Australia, New Zealand), with 12 control companies
- **Model**: 4-day week (typically 32 hours) with no pay reduction, preceded by work reorganisation
- **Findings**: Statistically significant improvements in burnout, job satisfaction, mental health, and physical health vs. controls
- **Mechanisms**: Better sleep, reduced fatigue, increased work ability
- **Dose-response**: Greater hour reductions predict larger wellbeing gains
- **Sustainability**: Over 90% of companies continued post-trial; benefits held at 12-month follow-up
- **Source**: [Nature Human Behaviour -- Prunster et al., 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-025-02259-6)

### 4 Day Week Global Combined Trials (2022-2024)

- **Scale**: 245+ organisations, 8,700+ employees across US, Canada, Ireland, UK, Australia, and more
- **Model**: Primarily 4-day, 32-hour week with no pay reduction
- **Employee rating**: 9.1 out of 10
- **Continuation**: 92% of companies continued
- **Burnout**: Nearly 70% reported less burnout
- **Mental health**: Over 40% said mental health improved
- **Sleep**: 38% experienced better sleep
- **Source**: [4 Day Week Global Research](https://www.4dayweek.com/research)

### Perpetual Guardian, New Zealand (2018)

- **Scale**: ~240 employees, 8-week trial
- **Model**: 4-day week with full pay
- **Work-life balance**: Jumped from 54% to 78% satisfaction
- **Stress**: Decreased from 45% to 38%
- **Productivity**: Increased by 20%
- **Outcome**: Made permanent in November 2018
- **Source**: [World Economic Forum](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2018/07/four-day-work-week-productivity-new-zealand-trial/)

### Microsoft Japan (August 2019)

- **Scale**: 2,300 employees, 1-month trial
- **Model**: Fridays off with full pay
- **Productivity**: Increased 39.9% (measured by sales per employee vs. August 2018)
- **Satisfaction**: 92.1% liked the 4-day workweek
- **Electricity use**: Decreased 23.1%
- **Printing**: Decreased 58.7%
- **Source**: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2019/11/04/776163853/microsoft-japan-says-4-day-workweek-boosted-workers-productivity-by-40)

### Valencia, Spain (April-May 2023)

- **Scale**: ~360,000 workers
- **Model**: Four weeks of 4-day work (leveraging existing holidays plus one additional day off)
- **Stress**: 35% reduction in stress levels
- **Sleep**: 64% increased their sleep
- **Social life**: 72% devoted more time to friends
- **Exercise**: 37% noted a rise in physical activity
- **Self-perceived health**: Higher status reported
- **Happiness**: Participants felt happier and more personally satisfied
- **Environmental**: Reduction in nitrogen dioxide emissions and improved air quality
- **Source**: [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/next/2023/10/31/workers-in-valencias-4-day-week-trial-report-less-stress-and-more-socialising)

### Germany (2023-2024, with 2026 Follow-Up)

- **Scale**: 45 organisations across diverse sectors
- **Model**: Reduced working time with no pay reduction
- **Findings**: Improvements in life satisfaction, sleep, physical activity, and employer attractiveness
- **Continuation**: 70% of organisations continue with reduced working time at 2-year follow-up; 22% further adapted their model
- **Source**: [4 Day Week Global -- Germany Pilot Results](https://www.4dayweek.com/germany-2024-pilot-results)

### Healthcare Leaders, USA (2024)

- **Scale**: 38 healthcare leaders at 2 U.S. hospitals, 4-month pilot
- **Burnout**: Decreased from 61% to 4%
- **Job satisfaction**: Increased from 71% to 96%
- **Joy in work**: Rose from 34% to 86%
- **Patient safety**: No adverse changes
- **Sustainability**: Improvements sustained at 6 and 12 months
- **Source**: [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40075240/)

## 8. The Cost of Overwork: Why This Matters

### WHO/ILO Data on Long Working Hours (2021)

The World Health Organization and International Labour Organization published a landmark joint analysis establishing the severe health risks of overwork:

- Long working hours caused **745,000 deaths** from stroke and ischaemic heart disease in 2016, a **29% increase since 2000**
- **398,000 people died from stroke** and **347,000 from heart disease** as a result of working 55+ hours per week
- Working 55+ hours/week is associated with a **35% higher risk of stroke** and **17% higher risk of dying from ischaemic heart disease** compared to working 35-40 hours
- Deaths from heart disease due to long hours **increased 42%** between 2000 and 2016; stroke deaths increased 19%
- **72% of deaths occurred among males**
- **9% of the global population** currently works long hours, and this percentage is increasing
- Long working hours are responsible for **approximately one-third of the total estimated work-related burden of disease**, making it the occupational risk factor with the largest disease burden

**Source**: [WHO -- Long Working Hours Increasing Deaths from Heart Disease and Stroke](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2021-long-working-hours-increasing-deaths-from-heart-disease-and-stroke-who-ilo)

### The Business Case for Mental Health Investment

- The WHO estimates that **every $1 invested in mental health treatment returns $4 in improved health and productivity** (WHO, cited by 4 Day Week Global).
- **22% of APA survey respondents** said their employer offered a 4-day workweek in 2024, compared with just 14% in 2022 -- adoption is accelerating (APA Work in America Survey, 2024).
- Working more than 40 hours per week is associated with **increased risk of burnout symptoms**, including emotional exhaustion and cynicism (UKRI, 2023).
